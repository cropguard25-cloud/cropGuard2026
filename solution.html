How CropGuard Works

CropGuard uses a simple, adaptive process to discourage birds from damaging crops while minimizing predictability and disturbance.

1. Detection

CropGuard uses visual monitoring to observe crop areas and identify bird activity that poses a risk to crops.

2. Adaptive Response

When bird activity is detected, CropGuard activates deterrence mechanisms such as randomized distress sounds and varied scare tactics.

The response is intentionally unpredictable to reduce the chance of birds becoming accustomed to it.

3. Adaptation Over Time

By varying timing, sound patterns, and response intensity, CropGuard aims to apply sustained pressure that discourages birds from returning to the protected area.

Design Principles

  • Detection-driven, not constant operation
  • Reduced habituation through variability
  • Affordable and modular design
  • Offline-first and solar-friendly operation

CropGuard is an early-stage concept. System design and features may evolve as development and testing progress.
